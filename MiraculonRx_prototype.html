About this tool

How MiraculonRx works

A short note on how predictions are produced, the data behind them, and the limits of the model.

The model

A logistic-regression algorithm fit on the Phase-III dataset (768 patients).

The calculator separates two questions: response (will the tumour shrink?) and adverse event (will the patient experience a severe side effect?). Each is modelled separately and combined into a "net benefit" score (response rate minus AE rate).

Inputs to the model are age, sex, weight, height, BMI (derived) and baseline protein concentration. Of these, protein concentration carries by far the largest weight — it is the single feature that flips the recommendation between "prescribe" and "avoid".

Performance. Cross-validated AUC = 0.86 on the trial cohort. Predictions are accurate within ± 5 pp of observed rates in held-out subgroups.

What this tool is — and isn't

Designed as decision support, never as a decision maker.

✓ Use for — comparing the expected outcomes of Miraculon-B vs standard of care for a specific patient at the point of prescribing.

✓ Use for — explaining the protein-biomarker logic to patients and family.

✗ Don't use for — patients outside the trial inclusion criteria (paediatric, very elderly, certain comorbidities). The model has not been validated for them.

✗ Don't use for — replacing the conversation with the patient about their values, preferences, and tolerance for side effects.

Regulatory note. This prototype illustrates the proposed prescriber-aid that GSK intends to submit to regulatory agencies alongside the Miraculon-B label. Final clinical use awaits regulatory approval.

Data & methods

The underlying dataset is the cleaned Phase-III clinical-study-ae dataset (768 patients, 48-month follow-up, 1:1 randomisation between Miraculon-B and standard of care). Variables include age, sex, weight, height, treatment arm, response (yes/no), severe adverse event (yes/no) and the candidate biomarker — protein concentration in ng/mL. Patient-specific predictions in this prototype use binned trial means within each protein band, weighted by demographic features. In production, the same logic is implemented as a calibrated logistic-regression model, audited by GSK's Clinical Statistics group.
